I only find within my bones A taste for eating earth and stones. When I feed, I feed on air, Rocks and coals and iron ore. My hunger, turn. Hunger, feed, A field of bran. Gather as you can the bright Poison weed. Eat the rocks a beggar breaks, The stones of ancient churches' walls; Pebbles, children of the flood, Loaves left lying in the mud. Beneath the bush a wolf will howl Spitting bright feathers From his feast of fowl: Like him, I devour myself.
